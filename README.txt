# Financial Distress and ESG Performance Analysis

> **Applied Data Science Project**  
> Does financial health predict ESG risk exposure? A firm-level empirical study on US public companies (2018–2021).

---

## 📋 Overview

This project investigates the relationship between **financial distress** and **ESG (Environmental, Social, Governance) risk incidents** for US-listed firms. We use the **Altman Z-score** as a proxy for financial health and a weighted ESG incident score derived from **RepRisk** data.

The analysis spans four years (2018–2021) and covers thousands of firm-year observations, after excluding financial (SIC 6000–6999) and utility (SIC 4900–4999) firms.

**Key finding:** A cross-sectional association exists between Z-score and ESG incident exposure, but this relationship disappears within firms over time — suggesting it is driven by stable firm characteristics (size, industry, management quality) rather than a direct financial distress mechanism.

## 🔬 Methodology

### Data Sources
- **RepRisk** — ESG risk incident database (firm-level, incident-level)
- **Financial data** — Compustat-sourced variables (total assets, retained earnings, EBIT, market cap, sales, liabilities)

### ESG Score Construction
Each incident is assigned a **weighted score** = `severity × reach`, then aggregated at the firm-year level for each ESG dimension (E, S, G) and in total. A simple count score is also computed for robustness checks.

### Financial Distress: Altman Z-score
The classic **Altman (1968)** formula for public firms:

$$Z = 1.2 \cdot \frac{WC}{TA} + 1.4 \cdot \frac{RE}{TA} + 3.3 \cdot \frac{EBIT}{TA} + 0.6 \cdot \frac{MktCap}{TL} + 1.0 \cdot \frac{Sales}{TA}$$

| Zone | Z-score |
|---|---|
| 🔴 Distress | Z < 1.81 |
| 🟡 Grey | 1.81 ≤ Z ≤ 2.99 |
| 🟢 Safe | Z > 2.99 |

### Analysis Pipeline

1. **Pre-processing** — date parsing, ESG indicator encoding, ISIN/CUSIP linking, 1-year lag on financials
2. **Descriptive Statistics** — distributions, winsorization (1st–99th percentile), time trends
3. **Correlation Analysis** — Spearman rank correlations + scatter plots
4. **OLS Regression** — baseline and extended (firm controls + year & industry FEs)
5. **Year-by-Year Regression** — coefficient stability over time
6. **Panel Data Models** — Pooled OLS vs Two-Way Fixed Effects (firm + year), revealing the cross-sectional nature of the relationship

## 📊 Main Results

| Model | β on Z-score | p-value |
|---|---|---|
| Baseline OLS | Negative | < 0.001 |
| + Firm controls (size, leverage, ROA) | Sign flip (positive) | Significant |
| + Year & Industry FEs | Positive | Significant |
| Two-Way FE (firm + year) | ~0 | Not significant |

> **Simpson's Paradox:** Firm size is a confounder — larger firms have both higher Z-scores and more ESG incidents (due to greater media scrutiny). Once controlled for, the baseline negative coefficient reverses. The within-firm FE result confirms the relationship is structural, not dynamic.

---

## 📝 Notes

- Financial firms (SIC 6000–6999) and utilities (SIC 4900–4999) are excluded, as the Altman Z-score formula is not applicable to these sectors.
- All continuous variables are winsorized at the 1st and 99th percentile to limit the influence of extreme values.
- Financial data is lagged by one year relative to ESG scores (financial health at year *t* explains ESG exposure at year *t+1*).
